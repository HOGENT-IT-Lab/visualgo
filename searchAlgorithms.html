Searching Algorithms

Sequential search

Suppose your program keeps a long array of names, say the names of 5000 students, where the names are not sorted. If you want to know whether your own name appears in the array, the most obvious way to find out is to go through all the names in the list one by one (front to back) and see if your name appears. This way of working is called LINEAIR SEARCH or SEQUENTIAL SEARCH.
